American Traditional Tattoos

Bold lines. Solid color. Imagery that's looked good for a hundred years and isn't stopping now.

American traditional is tattooing's foundation: heavy outlines, a deliberate color palette, and imagery that reads instantly — swallows, daggers, roses, moths, sacred icons. It's the style most proven to age well, and Carlos treats that tradition with respect.

Whether you want a classic piece straight from the old-school canon or a traditional treatment of your own idea, the goal is the same: a tattoo that's clean at arm's length, bold across the room, and solid for life.

Questions

American Traditional Tattoos — FAQ

Why do traditional tattoos age so well?

Bold outlines and solid color saturation give the skin time to soften without the design losing its read. Fifty-year-old traditional tattoos are still legible — that's the whole point of the formula.

Can you do a traditional version of my own idea?

Absolutely. Bring the concept and I'll translate it into traditional language — simplified shapes, strong outline, classic palette.

Does traditional work in black and grey?

Yes. Black-and-grey traditional and Chicano-influenced devotional work keep the bold structure while trading color for smooth grey shading.
